TELEHEALTH CONSULTATION GUIDELINES FOR CANCER PATIENTS
=========================================================
Document ID: DOC-005
Last Updated: January 2024
Category: Telehealth Services

1. OVERVIEW OF TELEHEALTH SERVICES
Telehealth provides cancer patients with remote access to their healthcare team through video calls, phone calls, and secure messaging. Telehealth is available for:
- Follow-up consultations after treatment.
- Symptom management and triage.
- Medication refill requests and prescription management.
- Discussion of lab results and imaging reports.
- Nutritional counseling and psychosocial support.
- Second opinions on diagnosis or treatment plans.
- Pre-treatment education and preparation.
Telehealth visits do not replace all in-person visits. Physical examinations, biopsies, imaging studies, chemotherapy infusions, radiation therapy, and surgical procedures require in-person attendance.

2. SCHEDULING A TELEHEALTH APPOINTMENT
Patients can schedule telehealth appointments through:
- The patient portal (available 24/7).
- Calling the oncology clinic during business hours (Mon-Fri, 8 AM - 5 PM).
- Requesting through the secure messaging system.
Telehealth appointments are typically available within 2-3 business days. Urgent symptom consultations may be available same-day. Patients should have their medication list, recent lab results, and a list of questions ready before the appointment.

3. MEDICATION REFILL THROUGH TELEHEALTH
Patients can request medication refills through telehealth consultations if:
- The medication is already prescribed and part of the current treatment plan.
- The medication does not require an in-person evaluation or physical examination.
- The patient has had a recent follow-up visit (within the past 3-6 months depending on the medication).
Controlled substances and certain high-risk medications may require an in-person visit for refill. Anti-nausea medications, pain management refills (non-opioid), hormone therapy medications, and supportive care medications can typically be refilled via telehealth.

4. WHEN TO SEEK IN-PERSON OR EMERGENCY CARE
Cancer patients should seek immediate in-person care or go to the emergency room if experiencing:
- Fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher, especially during chemotherapy (neutropenic fever risk).
- Severe bleeding or uncontrolled bleeding.
- Sudden severe chest pain or difficulty breathing.
- Signs of blood clot: sudden leg swelling, pain, or redness.
- Severe allergic reaction to medication.
- Confusion, seizures, or loss of consciousness.
- Severe dehydration with inability to keep fluids down for 24+ hours.
- New onset of severe pain not controlled by prescribed medications.
Do not delay seeking emergency care. Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room for life-threatening symptoms.

5. INSURANCE AND TELEHEALTH COVERAGE
Most insurance plans now cover telehealth visits for cancer care, including:
- Medicare and Medicaid cover telehealth visits for oncology care.
- Private insurance plans generally cover telehealth at the same rate as in-person visits.
- Copays for telehealth visits are typically the same as in-person visit copays.
- Some insurance plans may waive copays for telehealth visits. Check with your insurance provider.
- Financial assistance may be available for patients without adequate telehealth coverage.

6. TECHNOLOGY REQUIREMENTS FOR TELEHEALTH
To participate in a telehealth visit, patients need:
- A smartphone, tablet, or computer with a camera and microphone.
- A stable internet connection.
- A quiet, private space for the consultation.
- The clinic's telehealth app or platform (available for download from the patient portal).
Technical support is available by calling the IT help desk at the clinic during business hours.
